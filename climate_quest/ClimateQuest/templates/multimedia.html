The Greenhouse Effect

The greenhouse effect is a natural process that warms the Earth’s surface. When the Sun’s energy reaches the Earth, some of it is absorbed and re-emitted as heat. Greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), and water vapor (H₂O), trap this heat in the atmosphere, preventing it from escaping into space. This process keeps the Earth’s temperature stable and supports life. However, human activities like burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes have increased the concentration of greenhouse gases, intensifying the greenhouse effect and leading to global warming. This enhanced greenhouse effect is a major driver of climate change, resulting in rising temperatures, extreme weather events, and shifting ecosystems.
